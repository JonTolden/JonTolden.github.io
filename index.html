My Story

My journey began in the military, where I served as an MQ-1C Gray Eagle operator. I wasn’t just flying drones; I was part of a team where mission success depended on trust, timing, and technology working in harmony. During that time, I worked alongside engineers who spoke the language of circuits and systems. I found myself wanting to understand the "why" behind the machines we relied on.

After leaving the Army, I pursued electrical engineering to follow that interest. The shift from deployment zones to classrooms wasn’t always easy, but every circuit, simulation, and design project gave me a clearer sense of purpose. Engineering became more than a degree; it became a way to grow, solve meaningful problems, and apply the same discipline I carried in the military to creating technology that makes a difference.

Projects

The Caladrius Project (Complete)

Project Caladrius (HAVOC-03) was a long-endurance fixed-wing UAV developed over a 10-week rapid RDT&E cycle. Working with a 25-member interdisciplinary team, I led efforts in CAD modeling, FEA simulation, embedded system development, and machine learning integration to design, validate, and deploy the platform. The project showcased a balance of engineering depth and system-level execution, from structural and aerodynamic analysis to onboard avionics and autonomy development. Caladrius ultimately delivered a modular, flight-ready UAV that advanced endurance, adaptability, and the use of intelligent systems in real-world applications.

STM32 Bluetooth Developer Board (Complete)

I designed a custom Bluetooth-enabled STM32 development board as both a learning platform and a foundation for future embedded systems projects. Motivated by a mix of academic growth and personal interest in UAVs and IoT, the project strengthened my skills in PCB design, firmware engineering, and wireless communications. Using KiCAD 9.0 for schematic capture and PCB layout, along with STM32CubeMX/IDE and HAL libraries for microcontroller configuration and firmware development, I built a modular board that integrated Bluetooth connectivity, multiple I/O interfaces, and robust power regulation. The result was a functional, open-source dev board that supported rapid prototyping, experimentation, and real-world system integration.

Multi Rotor Axial Flux Motor (In Progress)

I’m working in a small team to develop a multirotor axial flux motor optimized for high torque and efficiency, targeting applications in electric propulsion and UAV systems. This project entails extensive computational electromagnetics simulations using EMWorks, Elmer, FEMM, and SimScale to analyze flux paths, thermal behavior, and rotor–stator interactions. By iterating through simulations and design refinements, I’m focusing on maximizing power density, reducing losses, and validating the feasibility of scalable multirotor configurations. The goal is to create a next-generation motor platform that blends innovative geometry with rigorous simulation-driven engineering for real-world performance.

VESC-Based Electronic Speed Controller (In Progress)

I’m independently designing a custom VESC-based high-power electronic speed controller (ESC) engineered to operate at upwards of 600V and 600A for advanced axial flux motor applications. Built on a foundation of SiC MOSFET technology and leveraging VESC firmware, the project focuses on achieving high switching efficiency, thermal stability, and reliable current control at up to 50 kHz. Tools such as KiCAD for PCB design and circuit/thermal simulations guide development, while features like liquid cooling, bus and phase current sensing, and support for CAN, UART, and encoder inputs ensure robustness. This ESC pushes beyond standard VESC limits to deliver scalable, high-performance control for next-generation propulsion and EV systems.
