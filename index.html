My Story

My journey began in the military, where I served as an MQ-1C Gray Eagle operator. I wasn’t just flying drones; I was part of a team where mission success depended on trust, timing, and technology working in harmony. During that time, I worked alongside engineers who spoke the language of circuits and systems. I found myself wanting to understand the "why" behind the machines we relied on.

After leaving the Army, I pursued electrical engineering to follow that interest. The shift from deployment zones to classrooms wasn’t always easy, but every circuit, simulation, and design project gave me a clearer sense of purpose. Engineering became more than a degree; it became a way to grow, solve meaningful problems, and apply the same discipline I carried in the military to creating technology that makes a difference.

DRV Power Protection & Monitoring PCB — Complete

Custom 4-layer inline board for 8S DRV motor-control systems, built to improve protection, monitoring access, and serviceability during bring-up and hardware testing.

Date Role Tools
Spring 2026 PCB Design Lead KiCad, JLCPCB

Problem / objective

Designed a removable inline board between the battery path and DRV-based motor-control hardware to add protection, sensing access, and safer troubleshooting without complicating system integration.

Your contribution

Owned the full lifecycle: schematic capture, 4-layer layout, component selection, fabrication handoff, ordering, debugging/rework, and validation planning. Supported schematic and layout review with teammates.

Design / implementation

Used a 1-input / 1-output inline architecture rated for up to 8S lithium, with dedicated battery, current, and temperature sensing access. The layout emphasized high-current routing, grounded monitoring paths, heatsinked power-device placement, and connectorized service points for bench validation.

Validation / results

Released through JLCPCB, fabricated/assembled, and taken through electrical checkout, integration, and hardware system testing. The final board established a reusable protection and monitoring stage for DRV-based ESC development.

Key design decisions

  • 4-layer stackup to separate power routing from monitoring returns.
  • Connectorized battery, current, and temperature sense points.
  • Service-oriented inline architecture for faster bench testing and fault isolation.

What this project proves

  • Protection-oriented PCB design for power hardware.
  • High-current layout with monitoring and service access.
  • Bring-up, debugging, and validation ownership.
