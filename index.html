This portfolio website highlights my path from U.S. Army MQ-1C UAS Operator to Electrical Engineer, blending hands-on projects with personal passion. It captures past achievements while presenting a forward-looking vision for innovation in aerospace, power systems, and emerging technologies.

My Story

My journey began in the military, where I served as an MQ-1C Gray Eagle operator. I wasn’t just flying drones; I was part of a team where mission success depended on trust, timing, and technology working in harmony. During that time, I worked alongside engineers who spoke the language of circuits and systems. I found myself wanting to understand the "why" behind the machines we relied on.

After leaving the Army, I pursued electrical engineering to follow that interest. The shift from deployment zones to classrooms wasn’t always easy, but every circuit, simulation, and design project gave me a clearer sense of purpose. Engineering became more than a degree; it became a way to grow, solve meaningful problems, and apply the same discipline I carried in the military to creating technology that makes a difference.

Stackable ESP32 RF Communication Module for ESC Systems — Complete

Compact add-on PCB built to add wireless control, board-to-board communication, and telemetry-ready expansion to a senior-design ESC platform.

Date Role Tools
Spring 2026 PCB Design Lead KiCad, ESP32, Arduino IDE

Problem / objective

Create a stackable ESP32 module that adds wireless joystick reception and control/data exchange to the ESC system without redesigning the main board.

Your contribution

Owned schematic capture, PCB layout, connector mapping, assembly, programming/bring-up, debugging, manual rework, and wireless/system validation.

Design / implementation

Built around an ESP32 with stackable headers, USB-C access, and board-to-board connectors for remote control and future telemetry. The layout prioritized RF-module placement, connector access, and rework-friendly routing for fast bring-up.

Validation / results

Board was fabricated, assembled, programmed, wirelessly tested, integrated with joystick control, and exercised with the larger motor-control system. USB data routing was recovered by direct jumper wiring from the ESP32 pins, and a redundant reverse-polarity MOSFET path was removed and replaced with a solder bridge.

What this project proves

  • Embedded/RF hardware integration
  • Modular stackable PCB architecture
  • Bring-up, rework, and system troubleshooting

Key design decisions

  • Stackable connector layout for ESC expansion
  • USB-C access for faster firmware bring-up
  • Targeted rework path to recover routing issues

BQ25570 Pendulum Energy Harvester PCB — Complete

Configurable energy-harvesting PCB built to rectify multi-coil pendulum-generator inputs, manage low-power startup, and store harvested energy for experimental loads and sensing.

Date Role Tools
Spring 2026 PCB Design Lead KiCad, LTspice, BQ25570

Problem / objective

Create a compact board that interfaces multiple pendulum-generator coils, supports configurable series/parallel routing, and converts intermittent low-level AC energy into a usable storage/output path.

Your contribution

Owned the architecture, schematic capture, PCB layout, rectifier integration, BQ25570 power stage, connector mapping, component selection, and design iteration for the harvester platform.

Design / implementation

Organized six coil channels into a low-loss rectifier front end feeding a BQ25570 harvester stage, storage-capacitor footprint, and output headers. The layout prioritized accessible coil connectors, short power paths, and a test-friendly architecture for comparing coil combinations.

Validation / results

The design consolidated rectification, harvesting, storage, and output routing into a single PCB, reducing off-board wiring and creating a repeatable platform for pendulum-energy experiments and future supercapacitor/load testing.

What this project proves

  • Analog/power PCB design for energy harvesting
  • Configurable multi-input rectifier architecture
  • Experimental hardware iteration and system integration

Key design decisions

  • Six-input coil interface for configurable routing tests
  • BQ25570-based harvester stage for low-power startup and storage control
  • Connector-first layout for rapid bench iteration

Work includes iterative electromagnetic and thermal simulation (EMWorks, Elmer, FEMM, SimScale), rotor-stator optimization, and validation of scalable multi-rotor geometry for improved power density.

DRV Power Protection & Monitoring PCB — Complete

Designed a custom 4-layer inline board for 8S DRV motor-control systems to improve protection, sensing access, and serviceability during bring-up and hardware testing. The architecture supports connectorized battery/current/temperature monitoring, high-current routing with thermal separation, and reusable inline fault-isolation for bench validation workflows.

Project highlights: 4-layer board, up to 8S lithium support, connectorized sensing points, and service-oriented inline architecture for faster bench validation and fault isolation.

VESC-Based High-Power ESC — In Progress

Designing a custom VESC-based electronic speed controller targeting up to 600V/600A operation for advanced axial flux motor systems. The architecture leverages SiC MOSFETs, high-frequency switching, thermal-aware PCB design, liquid-cooling integration, and robust sensing/control interfaces for CAN, UART, and encoder-driven applications.
