My Story

My journey began in the military, where I served as an MQ-1C Gray Eagle operator. I wasn’t just flying drones; I was part of a team where mission success depended on trust, timing, and technology working in harmony. During that time, I worked alongside engineers who spoke the language of circuits and systems. I found myself wanting to understand the "why" behind the machines we relied on.

Leaving the military and stepping into engineering school was a challenge. I went from deployment zones to classrooms, trading a uniform for textbooks and lab equipment. It was unfamiliar territory, but every circuit I wired and every system I modeled gave me a stronger sense of direction. Engineering became more than a career choice. It became a way to grow, to solve meaningful problems, and to continue serving—just in a different way.

Today, I approach every project with the same mindset I carried in the military: be precise, stay reliable, and keep improving. Whether I am working on UAV power systems, embedded controls, or high-voltage electric vehicle motors, I carry a deep respect for the impact engineering can make in the real world. My mission now is to build things that work when it matters most.

Projects

Project Caladrius – Long-Endurance UAV

Led electrical integration for a Group 2 UAV platform with onboard RGB-D, EO/IR, LiDAR, and comms systems. Designed power distribution and signal routing for endurance and payload compatibility.

400V VESC Redesign

Developed a custom ESC using SiC MOSFETs and digital isolation for high-performance axial flux EV motors. Handled schematic capture, PCB design in KiCAD, and system testing.
